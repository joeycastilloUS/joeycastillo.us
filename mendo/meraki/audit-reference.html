Meraki Anti-Patterns — "ReSharper for Meraki"

What every network engineer should not do: the bad decisions, hacks, workarounds, and gotchas. Each is a candidate audit check. Goal — make a network airtight by flagging the stuff people get wrong.

The thesis: a great linter doesn't just check "is it valid" — it knows the mistakes. This catalog turns 40 years of "don't do that" into checks. green = already in the audit, amber = new, to build. The biggest gap is Switching — almost entirely unbuilt and where the worst footguns live.

1. The #1 Meraki gotcha (must surface loudly)

Port-forwards and 1:1 NAT BYPASS the L3 firewall. Engineers add an L3 "deny" thinking it protects an exposed service — it doesn't. Inbound port-forward / NAT traffic is allowed regardless of L3 outbound rules. So an RDP port-forward is reachable from the whole internet even with a deny rule sitting right above it. NEW: when a port-forward exists, explicitly warn that L3 rules do NOT protect it — scope it on the rule's own allowed-IPs or use VPN.

Build order (highest value first)

  1. Switching category (SW-*) — native-VLAN-1, trunk-all, unused-enabled, DHCP guard, STP/BPDU/loop guard, storm control, port security. The biggest unguarded surface; needs switch/ports + switch settings reads.
  2. Port-forward bypass warning + EXP-04/05 — the highest-impact single gotcha.
  3. Wireless WIFI-03/06/07 (guest isolation, per-SSID LAN block, rogue AP/Air Marshal).
  4. Ops OPS-01/02/03 + VPN-01 (firmware, logging, alerts, S2S VPN).
  5. Org ORG-* (whole-org pass).
  6. Anomalies ANOM-03..06 (fold portmap + device statuses into the audit).
