The retro

What went well

  • Contract-first held. Proved the spine on ce at $0 before touching DE — no porting a moving target.
  • The thesis is real, not slideware. 6 tildes replay LLM-free, one tail not 32, decoder invariant byte-identical, one card → N dialects.
  • Fall-fast worked. A1's parser failed its first test; fixed in one pass, green — never repeated.
  • The full-court press earned its keep. Caught "the Catalog already exists" before building the wrong thing.
  • gcc actually compiles here. Re-verifying the "compile-gated" claim (Rule 13) upgraded ship-readiness.
  • Model policy paid off. Opus coded; Fable took A2/A4/A6/A7/A8; the one Opus first-test fail escalated correctly.

What could be better

  • I claimed "WDAC blocks compile" before testing — it doesn't, for isolated C. Verify the toolchain first.
  • Didn't survey existing be/pi/ cards until A3 — the operator-owned dialect (attendant.pi) surprised me late.
  • The DoD drift (8 vs 10) was a latent kernel inconsistency, only caught by the press.
  • The reference is Python; the production data_access.c parser for A1 isn't written yet.
  • decode_ids needs the nous header tree, absent from this sandbox.
  • Cards are seal: unsigned — sealing is unresolved pending ~seal + your ratify.
