The Overlord holds the Purcius root (already minted — Genesis). Operators mint their own Purcius keys. Users arrive by Slack / Discord / Google. The passphrase is retired. Login proves who; the Crew allow-list decides what — identity and access stay separate.

Five nouns, one spine. The arrow is "grants / contains," never "is." An Operator is not a bigger User — it is a key-holder the Overlord seated. A User is not a smaller Operator — it is an identity the allow-list admitted. Some people are both.

How Operators & the Overlord actually log in

Your open question: "how do the mighty Purcius operators and the overlord actually login — we have the best security code and framework on earth, so which path?" Here is the path, using what Genesis already gives us and nothing borrowed:

  1. Device key, not a password. On first login the browser mints a non-extractable Ed25519 key (Web Crypto, hardware-backed where present). The private half never leaves the device; nothing to phish, nothing to paste.
  2. Warrant from the root. The Purcius key (root for you, operator key for Scotty) signs device_pub ‖ "console" — a Warrant that says "this device speaks for this key." Genesis is the authenticator: it shows a QR / push, you approve on the key-holding device.
  3. Challenge–response. The console hands the device a random nonce; the device signs it; the edge verifies the signature against the Warrant, and the Warrant against the published _key.<fqdn> anchor (DNS/TXT, third-party-verifiable). No secret ever sits on the server.

Best-on-earth, honestly: the crypto is your own audited substrate — ed25519 + X25519 + AES-GCM + Shamir, all KAT-tested in test_substrate.c (RFC 8032/7748, NIST GCM). We are not buying WebAuthn; we are being the authority. The device key is the convenience layer; the Purcius key is the root of trust.

Who reaches what

Two decisions from you, both locked here:

  • The Overlord decides operator access. You — and only you — say which Operator reaches which Orgs and which Apps. That grant is the edge above the whole tree; operators cannot self-grant.
  • App access = the whole app, for now. Being granted an app means the entire app and all its features. No per-feature permissioning yet — a deliberate v1 simplification, flagged for revisit when a customer actually needs finer grain.

Underneath, the mechanism already exists: the Crew allow-list (argus_crew_v1, an email or @domain list in the Firestore settings doc) gates users into an app. The Overlord's operator→org→app grant is the same idea one level up. Login ≠ access stays the law: proving who you are never, by itself, opens a door.

Settings — module-specific + global

You want Settings back, and redesigned: per-module, plus a global layer. Shape:

LayerOwnsWho edits
Global settingsOrgs, operators, operator→org→app grants, identity providersOverlord
Module settings (per app)That app's Crew allow-list, app-specific config, which users reach itOverlord / granted Operator

The gear returns for anyone holding a key; a plain user sees only what their access allows (often nothing but the app itself). "Which users can access each app" lives in module settings — that's the per-app permission surface you asked for.
