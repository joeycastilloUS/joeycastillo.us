High-Level Summary

Sizes the 12 phase-boards (P0-P11) of the metal.de <-> metal.ce parity plan into user stories: the Desktop Edition executing the full tilde contract natively — Tier-0 deterministic C at $0.00 (route_tilde -> birt_dispatch -> tier0_exec), escalating to the Pantheon only where synthesis is real — inside the fresh consolidated kastil-systems/metal-desktop repo, with the four organs (board_status.c, report_emit.c, telemetry, signal_emit.c) making DE a full citizen of the Be discipline. Note: the plan's phase table itself sums to 128 pts (the footer's ~115 undercounts its own 12 rows); this decomposition lands at 132 — the +4 delta is P2 (+1, the seam proof honestly splits into two 3s) and P9 (+3, a mandatory Rule-13 infra-verification sibling story).

The shape of it: 12 deliverables take metal’s Desktop Edition to full parity with the Console Edition. The first three (P0–P2) are the whole bet — approve the spec, stand up the repo, and prove one tilde runs natively at $0.00. The middle (P4–P6) wires the “organs” that make DE a real citizen of the discipline. P7–P8 harden it; P10 lands the first tilde→binary binding; P11 declares parity with receipts. Only P9 is blocked (waiting on an external prime/relay deploy) and it’s built so it never blocks the rest.

Sequencing & Dependencies

P0 gates everything — no board is pulled before Joey approves the *.proposed spec, and if the origin re-baseline moves the 13-point surface, plan v2 lands before P1 (a stated When-To-Revisit trigger). P0 -> P1 -> P2 -> P3 are strictly ordered: no repo, no seam; no seam, no verbs. After P3, P4/P5/P6 are interleave-eligible — the three organ sets (status/telemetry, reports, signals) are mutually independent but all consume the P2/P3 frame; in practice P5's chain-order wiring wants P4's telemetry step present to be complete. P7 and P8 are parallel-eligible after P4 (auth touches serve.c + relay_client; cross-platform touches sdk/http + judge — disjoint files, safe in parallel). P10 needs P3's frame and realistically P5's report chain for its ~t reports. P9 is blocked on external prime/relay deployment and is calendar-independent: it slots in whenever prime lands and must NOT gate P10/P11 — its Rule-13 reachability story runs first the moment prime is claimed live. P11 is last and consumes everything unblocked; it explicitly records the P9 queue-only posture if prime hasn't landed.

Value Rationale

Order is blocker-removal first, then contract organs, then hardening, then the gate. P0 is rank 1 because it is pure risk-kill: a 3-point re-baseline can invalidate 120 downstream points, so it buys the most certainty per point of anything on the list. P1-P3 are the critical path to the thesis — a tilde running natively at $0.00 is the entire pitch, and P2's 6 points are the cheapest possible proof of the load-bearing architecture (if route_tilde + tier-0 can't round-trip near the promised ~40 LOC, we go back to ~d having spent 6 points, not 60). P4-P6 come next because emission is the contract: board-status, reports, telemetry, and Signals are what make DE a citizen of the discipline rather than a sibling cockpit — and they're wired from the first tilde, not bolted on. P7/P8 harden identity and platform floor once there's something worth securing and porting. P10 lands the canon-required first .pi binding before the gate. P9 ranks last not by value but by external dependency — it executes opportunistically whenever prime deploys. P11 closes with receipts.

Estimation Risks

  • CE origin drift is the master risk: the parity target was inventoried from a clone 275 commits behind, so P0's re-baseline can resize every downstream board — treat all points after P0 as conditional on the surface holding.
  • P1's history merge is the one effectively-irreversible move (subtree split out of kastil-systems/tools + unrelated-history merge under fresh org policies/Go.Sign); if org branch-protection or signing fights the merge, the 13 could stretch, and it cannot be cheaply redone once new revisions accrue.
  • P8's http_compat 5-pointer rests on 'one afternoon of source archaeology' (lift nous tls13 fill vs. complete CE's partial tls13.c) — if both lineages are broken off-Windows, this is the least trustworthy 13 on the list.
  • WDAC under automation taxes every board's ~t leg: metal-desktop.exe and gcc are blocked from Claude's sub-shell on Joey's box, so verify loops need interactive-terminal hand-off — friction that isn't in any single story's points but drags all of them.
