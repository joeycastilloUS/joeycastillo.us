Inspect — the Templates feature, honestly

VERDICT: the operator is right. This is a capability demo, not a feature. The attribution engine underneath is real and good; the surface answers no operator question and drives no operator action. And parts of it aren't even “done” — the attribution is partial, noisy, and dead-ends. I marked it COMPLETE on plumbing evidence, not use.

What the tab actually shows (from the operator's seat)

Open argus.com.ai/app → Zendesk → Templates. You get:

  • Chips: total templates, how many carry a body, hot/warm/dead counts.
  • Macros grouped by their Zendesk category, ranked by 30-day usage, with a usage bar and a DEAD flag for 0-in-30d.
  • A “⚑ attributed in N →” badge per template that expands to a few raw ticket numbers.

That is the whole feature. It is a read-only inventory with a fuzzy usage-ish badge.

Why it isn't useful

ProblemDetail
Answers no questionThere is no operator question this tab answers that they act on. “Here are your macros and how often they fired” is a fact, not a decision. What do I do after reading it? Nothing is offered.
Duplicates what existsZendesk's own admin already lists macros + usage. Worse: Argus's own Audit tab already grades dead macros (158/268 dead, with a remediation/apply lane). The Templates tab re-shows the same inventory with less action than the Audit tab it sits next to.
No action anywhereNothing is clickable-to-do. No archive, no “open in Zendesk,” no “these are duplicates,” no export. The sample ticket ids are plain text (#453811) — not even links.
No outcome“This template fired 40 times” says nothing about whether it worked (recovery, CSAT). Usage without outcome is vanity.

Why parts aren't “done” either

GapEvidence
Attribution is partialThe rollup scanned 40 of 298 candidate tickets (capped) over 30 days. “Attributed in 5” means “5 of the 40 we happened to scan” — not real usage. An operator reads it as a true count; it isn't.
Attribution is noisyLive rollup returned templates titled * (×5) and test (×1) as “attributed.” A macro literally named * matching agent replies is a false signal surfaced as fact.
Dead-endsThe badge expands to raw ticket numbers with no link and no next step. The trail stops exactly where an operator would want to go.
The engine is fineTo be fair: the C99 attribution engine is genuinely good (93.3% top-1, precision 1.00, Oracle tiers). The failure is entirely in what we did with it, not the matcher.

The root miss: no user, no job

The original design named three “needs”: see usage, see how programs use templates, show the demos using them. Every one of those is “see” — a viewing verb, not a doing verb. We built exactly what was asked and it's still not useful, because the need was never tied to a decision a named operator makes. “See your templates” was the wrong target; it survived all the way to production because nothing in the pipeline forced the question “and then the operator does what?”

Where the value actually is (feeds the plan)

The attribution engine is a real asset if pointed at a real decision. Candidates, each a doing:

  • Off-script / template-gap detection — find replies that were hand-written where a good template existed (or where none did). That is a coaching signal and a “we need a macro for X” signal. The engine's NOT-MATCHED + near-miss data already knows this.
  • Template hygiene as a worklist — dead + duplicate + near-duplicate templates → an archive/merge worklist that fires through the Audit tab's existing remediation lane. Action, not a list.
  • Outcome tie — which templates correlate with recovery (needs the NPS outcome data from the NPS-LIVE work). The highest value, but blocked on that data.

The plan (~p) picks one, ties it to a named operator + a decision, and kills the passive list.

Open questions for the plan

  • Who is the operator for this — a GR lead cleaning up macros, or a coach watching agents go off-script? Different features.
  • Does the Templates tab survive at all, or does it fold into the Audit tab (which already owns macro grading + remediation)?
  • Is the attribution worth a full scan (all 298+), or is per-ticket-on-demand (the ticket you're looking at) the real use, not a rollup?

Definition of Done

  • ✓ Assessed from the operator's vantage, not the endpoint's — named what it shows, why it's not useful, and where it isn't done
  • ✓ Evidence-backed (40/298 cap, */test noise, Audit-tab overlap, dead-end badge)
  • ✓ Named the root miss (no user/no decision) and the real value candidates for ~p
  • ✓ No spin — the “COMPLETE” stamp was wrong; retro (~r) owns why
