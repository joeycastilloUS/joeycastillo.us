Retro — Argus on Gemini/Vertex, grounded on the .ids

One ~d~p~e~b~t~s~r run, three services, 21 pts, all shipped live. Argus is off Anthropic and answering from the real corpus. Here's what held, what didn't, and what becomes next work.

What went well

  • Reuse-first was the whole game. The recon (Gemini/Vertex already live in gcp-bridge; IDS already queryable via onedine-raw) turned a "build an AI-over-tickets system" into a wiring job. 5 of 8 capabilities were already in production. Reading before building saved reinventing two hard things.
  • Parallel build across independent repos. Three agents, three repos, zero file conflicts — argus-slack + onedine-raw + gcp-bridge built concurrently, all clean (dotnet 0/0 ×2, C99 binary).
  • The verify loop earned its keep (Rule 7). Testing /ask live caught two real bugs the compilers never would: a multi-word query hitting a substring matcher (0 hits), and a case:-prefixed id breaking /replay (empty bodies). Both fixed and re-tested green before Done. "It compiles" would have shipped a silent liar.
  • It never hallucinated. Under 8 real questions, every miss was an honest "not in these tickets" — the no-invent design held. That was the one non-negotiable.
  • Safe deploy order protected the operator. Data + bridge went live and were proven before the Anthropic cord was pulled, so Argus never went mute during the swap.
  • Third-party-verifiable proof without the Slack UI. A properly-signed message.im event exercised the entire wired path and posted a real DM — provable from logs, not vibes (Rule 9).

What could be better

  • Recall floor. ~5/8 find-questions retrieved tickets that didn't match the meaning. Substring + term-hit ranking finds words, not intent. Known, and the v2 spec.
  • Three Cloud Builds for one service. gcp-bridge deployed 3× (initial + 2 bug fixes) because the bugs surfaced only against live onedine-raw. A local integration harness would have caught both before the first deploy.
  • onedine-raw redeploy was fragile. Its deploy context (nous/ + raw-ids.tgz) isn't committed — I had to reconstruct it from GCS + the metal nous tree by hand. One typo = an empty-store ship.
  • Naive term extraction. Keyword-split loses intent; a cheap Gemini term-extract or query-rewrite pass would likely lift recall a lot for near-zero cost.
  • /ask cited all K, not the relevant ones. It grounds on 5 tickets even when only 2 are on-topic — a re-rank or a "drop irrelevant" pass would tighten citations.
