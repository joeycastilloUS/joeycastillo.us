The loop

Every phase of this migration runs as a four-step loop:

  1. Propose — You type a one-line prompt. AI reads the master plan + this page, drafts the exact commands for one phase, surfaces the risk, and shows the rollback for that phase only. No filesystem or cloud writes happen yet.
  2. Approve — You reply go (or push back: edit X, skip, kill). AI never proceeds without an explicit go.
  3. Execute — AI runs the commands. You see each tool call as it happens. AI does not chain into the next phase unprompted.
  4. Verify — AI runs the acceptance check from the plan. If green, AI tells you the phase is complete and waits. If red, AI surfaces the failure and proposes a remediation -- you approve again.

Repeat for the next phase. Loop ends when all 10 phases are green.

Kill switch

At any gate, you can abort the chain:
kill
or
stop — rollback the last phase

AI immediately stops proposing further phases. If rollback was requested, AI proposes the rollback step from the master plan for the most recent phase that introduced state changes (cloud or filesystem) and waits for your go on it.

The chain never advances autonomously. Every phase is gated.

What AI never does in this mode

  • Send messages to Wesley (or anyone) on its own
  • Force-push or amend commits
  • Drop a Snowflake user, role, or grant without your go
  • Delete a GCP secret without your go
  • Skip the verification step after an execute
  • Chain to the next phase without a fresh prompt from you
  • Read or transmit your passphrases (you type them at the openssl prompt; AI never sees them)

Trace level (lighter than TRON)

Each gate emits a single audit line to be/otel.jsonl with: phase, action, your approve/edit/skip/kill response, the resulting commit hash (if any). Enough to reconstruct what happened; not the full action-by-action trace TRON requires.
