Big 10 — massive threads reduced to pure intent

Ten of the biggest real Zendesk threads, one per use case, run through the real understand() + WordNet pipeline into a deterministic entities + actions + signals digest — the payload the judge assesses instead of raw prose.
PRIVATE — Mendocino Farms client data. Raw threads + full digests are staged locally and gitignored (never committed). Names redacted in the example below. This report is the method + manifest.

Why reduce before judging

A support thread can be 30 comments and 15,000 characters of quoted replies, signatures, and noise. Handing that raw to a judge model is expensive, non-deterministic, and full of PII. So first we do a deterministic, $0, pure-C pass: understand() reads the whole thread and extracts its intent skeleton — the unique entities (who/what), the actions (what's being done), and the structural signals (negation, causation, conditionals). The judge then assesses that skeleton.

Worked example — Supply Chain #341562 (names redacted)

A 19-comment vendor-credit thread (8,520 chars) reduces to this skeleton the judge can read at a glance:

ENTITIES (who / what)

[agent name]invoice numberus foods contact team [name] cherry creek mendo vendor supportservice request numberinvoice [store address]requesterfeedbackaccount

providerequestinvoicingresolved processedclosedaddedserviceemail

From 19 comments of back-and-forth, the intent is legible: a vendor (US Foods) credit/invoice request for the Cherry Creek store that moved through request → invoicing → resolved → closed. That is exactly what a judge needs to classify + route — with none of the quoted-reply noise.

Honest caveats (the cleanup pass next)

  • HTML noise leaks in — nbsp and markup fragments show up as "entities". A pre-strip of HTML entities kills these.
  • Function-ish verbs leak into actions — please, has, see, have. A stopword/aux filter on the ACTION list cleans it.
  • Structural signals came out sparse on threaded prose (mostly declarative status updates) vs. the crisp single sentences — worth a look at whether long lines truncate at the 1024-char intent limit.
  • These are datamap/filter improvements, not engine problems — the entity/action extraction itself is real, as the example shows.

Ready for judging

All ten digests are staged. The next step is the assessment: feed each intent digest (not the raw thread) to the BYO-BIG3 judge and have it classify the use case, sentiment, and next action — cheaper, deterministic-fed, PII-reduced. That's the ~b after this.
