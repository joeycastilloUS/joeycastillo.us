🏆 Government cert path — ALL IP-CLEAN, no surprises

Decisive finding: across every Western government cryptographic-conformance program reviewed — FIPS 140-3 / CAVP, NIAP CC, EUCC, CSPN (France), BSZ (Germany), JCMVP (Japan), KCMVP (Korea), JISEC, KECS, SCCS, IC3S (India), CCCS (Canada), MyCC (Malaysia), AISEP (Australia) — no program claims ownership of Joey's invention. They test conformance to a published standard. That's it.

EUCC even has explicit language protecting your IP — Reg (EU) 2024/482 Article 43:

"...protection of business secrets and other confidential information, including trade secrets, as well as preserving intellectual property rights."

The only 🔴 government cert to skip: OSCCA (China). No explicit IP-transfer clause but the PRC Cryptography Law + Cybersecurity Law create legal source-code-review mechanisms; foreign vendors are effectively barred from mainline. If you ever need China access, ship a separate ShangMi-algorithm fork from a Chinese subsidiary — never let mainline through.

The CSfC trophy — note the operational tax: The NSA CSfC Memorandum of Agreement transfers no IP, but binds the vendor to vuln-reporting SLAs, change-notification, and NSA's right to recommend removal from the Components List. Read the MoA carefully before signing. This is operational discipline, not IP risk.

The bottom line, revised

Round 1 recommended TC Disrupt + RSAC ISB + Pwn2Own as star events. Round 2's verbatim IP-clause extraction shows all three are hostile to Joey's IP-protective posture in different ways — Disrupt's submission license is broad property rights, RSAC ISB's new mandatory $5M SAFE is cap-table dilution, Pwn2Own is direct IP transfer.

The path the deep audit reveals: Every Western government cert is IP-clean (FIPS, CC, EUCC, CSPN — none claim your invention). The cleanest competitions are the recognition-only ones (EFF, Pwnies, USENIX, ISC2, RWC). The cleanest grants are NSF SBIR (best IP protection on the board) and Sovereign Tech Fund (curl is the exact precedent).

The 🔴 lesson: Startup-pitch competitions and vulnerability-bounty platforms have evolved aggressive IP terms over the past 5 years specifically because they're now monetizing submissions. Recognition-based awards (academic + industry recognition + standards-body conformance) have stayed clean because their business model doesn't depend on owning your work. Pursue recognition + conformance. Avoid pitch-license traps.
