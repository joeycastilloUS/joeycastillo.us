Michelle, this brief lays out the certifications, grants, and recognition opportunities the kastil-systems program will pursue over the next 36 months. The strategy is a three-layer stack:

  1. Trifecta of conformance certifications — FIPS 140-3, Common Criteria via NIAP, and ISO/IEC 27001:2022. These unlock procurement in roughly 35 nations via the CCRA mutual-recognition arrangement plus every Fortune 500 procurement gate.
  2. Trophy — NSA CSfC Components List inclusion. Achievable only after the Trifecta. Opens classified-mesh deployments that no competitor without it can bid on.
  3. Grant pipeline — NLnet, NSF SBIR, Sovereign Tech Fund, DARPA, DHS SVIP — these underwrite the laboratory fees so the heavy certifications are not paid out-of-pocket.

Underneath those three layers, a foundation of five free supply-chain trust badges (OpenSSF Best Practices, OpenSSF Scorecard, SLSA, SBOM, Sigstore) ship in weeks of engineering work with zero filing fees. Three recognition awards (EFF Pioneer, Pwnie Best Defense, ISC2 Mid-Career Achievement) round out the credibility surface.

§3 · The Trifecta — conformance certifications

FIPS 140-3 / CMVP — joint US + Canada validationPRIORITY 1

Issuing bodyNational Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), US Department of Commerce; jointly operated with Canadian Centre for Cyber Security (CCCS)
Subject of certificationlibsecurity.a — the pure-C99 cryptographic module (approximately 900 lines of code)
CostLaboratory fees: $50,000–$200,000 (depending on chosen CSTL and module complexity); NIST cost-recovery fees: approximately $16,000
TimelineApproximately 12–24 months end-to-end; current NIST review queue is 12–18 months. Re-certification every 5 years.
IP termsNone. The CMVP Management Manual contains no intellectual-property-assignment clause. Cryptographic Security Testing Laboratory (CSTL) is bound by commercial NDA. Source code remains the vendor's; only the publicly-authored Non-Proprietary Security Policy and the validation certificate number become public.
EligibilityAny vendor worldwide. CSTL must be NVLAP-accredited (labs in US, Canada, Germany, UK, Spain, Italy, Turkey, Japan, India).
PrerequisiteAll approved algorithms must hold CAVP/ACVP certificates first (~$5,000–$25,000 standalone; do this first as a separate filing)
URLhttps://csrc.nist.gov/projects/cryptographic-module-validation-program · Management Manual: FIPS 140-3 CMVP Management Manual
Recommended CSTLs (engage one)
  • atsec information security — https://www.atsec.com/contact-us/ — software-module specialty, FedRAMP-aware
  • Acumen Security — https://www.acumensecurity.net/contact/ — strong on NDcPP + FIPS bundle
  • Lightship Security — https://lightshipsec.com/contact/ — reputation for speed
Process(1) Engage CSTL via direct contact; (2) sign commercial NDA; (3) submit module + design documentation; (4) CSTL conducts algorithmic conformance testing (CAVP) and module testing; (5) CSTL writes test report; (6) report submitted to CMVP review queue; (7) NIST + CCCS issue joint validation certificate
Existing holders (cohort proof)Cisco, Palo Alto Networks, CrowdStrike Falcon, Microsoft Windows Crypto Provider, Apple CoreCrypto, Google BoringCrypto, Oracle, Red Hat, wolfSSL

Pre-flight checklist

  • Complete CAVP/ACVP certification first for: AES-256-GCM, SHA-256, HMAC-SHA-256, PBKDF2, Ed25519, X25519. Approximately $5,000–$25,000 standalone via the same CSTL.
  • Decide module scope: software-only Level 1 (recommended starting point) versus higher levels
  • Request CSTL quotes from at least two of the three recommended labs
  • Budget allocation: confirm funding source (NSF SBIR Phase II if Phase I succeeds, otherwise direct)
  • Prepare Non-Proprietary Security Policy template (will be drafted with CSTL during engagement)

§4 · The Trophy — NSA CSfC Components List inclusion

NSA Commercial Solutions for Classified (CSfC) Components ListTROPHY · 36 MONTHS

Issuing bodyNational Security Agency (NSA), Information Assurance Directorate (IAD)
AwardListing on the CSfC Components List, which qualifies the product for use in NSA-approved Capability Packages that handle classified data via layered commercial cryptography
CostFolded into FIPS 140-3 and Common Criteria certification costs above; no additional NSA fee
TimelineMulti-year. Achievable only AFTER both FIPS 140-3 and NIAP CC certifications are issued. Realistic target: 36 months from program inception.
IP termsNone transferred. The CSfC Memorandum of Agreement (MoA) contains no IP assignment clause. However: the MoA imposes operational obligations — vulnerability-reporting SLAs, change-notification requirements, and NSA's right to recommend removal from the Components List. EligibilityFIPS 140-3 + NIAP CC certificates must be in hand; product category must align with an active Capability Package
URLhttps://www.nsa.gov/Resources/Commercial-Solutions-for-Classified-Program/
Strategic valueThe CSfC Components List is the procurement door for classified-mesh deployments in US government and allied nations. A commercial competitor without this listing cannot bid on those contracts.

§8 · Risk register — opportunities to AVOID

The following opportunities have intellectual-property or equity terms that conflict with the kastil-systems sovereignty posture. Verbatim clauses are extracted from the issuing bodies' published terms.

Avoid: TechCrunch Disrupt Battlefield 200

Submission license attaches to every word of any application material:

"royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, unrestricted, transferable, sublicensable...worldwide license to use, copy, modify, transmit, sell, exploit, create derivative works...for any purpose that we choose"

Avoid: RSAC Innovation Sandbox

Two structural concerns:

  • $5 million Crosspoint Capital SAFE is now MANDATORY for all Top-10 finalists (became a cap-table commitment in November 2024, not just a prize)
  • 2025 AI-training clause added to terms: "RSAC may use Submission Materials to train AI Technologies...all Outputs shall be owned by RSAC"

Avoid: Pwn2Own (all editions)

Direct intellectual-property transfer per Zero Day Initiative researcher agreement:

"the exploits and whitepapers will become the property of the Sponsor in accordance with the ZDI researcher agreement" + Section 10: "All intellectual property...are owned by Sponsor and/or its affiliates."

Avoid: NIST PQC algorithm submission

Submission of an algorithm to the Post-Quantum Cryptography standardization process commits to a mandatory royalty-free worldwide license, permanently, with no withdrawal possible. Acceptable only if intentionally contributing an algorithm to the standards process. Implementing the standardized algorithms (Kyber, Dilithium, etc.) is unaffected and recommended.

Avoid: BIRD Cyber Foundation grants

Marketed as "no equity" but the agreement contains a conditional repayment clause: 30% of revenue OR 50% of sale-price, up to 1.5× the grant amount. Effectively a revenue-share investment rather than a grant.

Avoid: Geographically-walled programs (without restructuring)

  • Singapore Cybersecurity Capability Development Fund (CCDF) — "All IP must be owned, managed and exploited from Singapore"
  • Israel Innovation Authority (IIA) — Israeli legal entity required
  • Innovate UK — UK legal entity required
  • European Innovation Council Accelerator — EU SME registration required
  • EU European Defence Fund (EDF) — EU-only

Avoid: China OSCCA / MLPS

No explicit IP-transfer clause in program documentation; however, the People's Republic of China Cryptography Law and Cybersecurity Law create legal mechanisms for source-code review of foreign vendors. Foreign vendors are effectively barred from the mainline market. If China access is ever strategically required, ship a separate fork compiling against the ShangMi (SM2/SM3/SM4) algorithm suite from a Chinese subsidiary; do not feed the mainline product through.
