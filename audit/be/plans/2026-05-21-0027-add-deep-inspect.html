Reframe: the audit got the symptom right but the disease wrong.

The first audit said: "a-d-d exists in 6 places, that's drift." Reading the actual content changes the picture. There are four distinct roles across the 6 repos, and the real problem isn't duplication — it's that they all share a single name.

  1. The methodology source — kastil-systems/a-d-d (kASTIL-branded rules + NOUS architecture + target devices)
  2. The teaching/template surface — joeycastilloUS/a-d-d-guidance (verbose <PROJECT>-templated GLOBAL.md, PRIMER, CHANGELOG, docs.html)
  3. Per-context instantiations — acmedevUS/a-d-d (real Acme business architecture: Pampanga mesh, off-grid plans) and ai-pantheon-internal/ADD (real Pantheon NOUS purpose-learning + purpose-memory architecture)
  4. Empty starter shells — Life-Water/a-d-d (5 files, placeholders), acmedevUS/a-d-d-template (already archived)

None of these should be deleted or "redirected" the same way. The work is to rename three of them — because a-d-d as a name belongs to ONE thing (the methodology), not to "any folder organized in the a-d-d shape."
