## 1) US Federal — Defense / Intel / Research

### 1.1 DARPA HARDEN (Hardening Development Toolchains Against Emergent Execution Engines)
- **Funder:** DARPA Information Innovation Office (I2O)
- **Award size:** Multi-million per performer; HARDEN cohort awards historically $5-15M per multi-org team across 48 months
- **What it funds:** Tools, metadata, and instrumentation that prevent "weird machine" emergent execution. Direct fit for a pure-C toolchain claim — libsecurity.a's "no exploitable surface" thesis maps perfectly
- **Eligibility:** Open to industry incl. small business; teaming with a Boston University / Riverside Research / BedRock-style partner expected. Sole-founder org can subcontract under a prime
- **Process:** HARDEN cohort already selected; future work via DARPA I2O office-wide BAA (rolling). Unsolicited proposals accepted any time via the I2O BAA
- **Deadline:** Rolling (I2O office-wide BAA renews annually)
- **Strategic value:** DARPA logo permanent on the CV. Unlocks DoD pipeline. The "HARDEN-adjacent" prose alone is fundable elsewhere
- **Source:** https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/hardening-development-toolchains-against-emergent-execution-engines · https://www.darpa.mil/research/opportunities/baa

### 1.2 DARPA INGOTS (Intelligent Generation of Tools for Security)
- **Funder:** DARPA I2O
- **Award size:** Multiple-award contract; performer awards typically $1-5M
- **What it funds:** AI + program analysis to find chainable high-severity vulnerabilities before exploit. libsecurity.a's small surface plus AI-assisted code-audit pitch is a clean angle
- **Eligibility:** Same as HARDEN — open with teaming
- **Process:** Proposers Day already happened (2023); contract presolicitation issued 2024. Submit via SAM.gov when next BAA opens, or under the standing I2O BAA
- **Source:** https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/intelligent-generation-tools-security

### 1.3 DARPA AI Cyber Challenge (AIxCC) follow-on funding
- **Funder:** DARPA + ARPA-H
- **Award size:** Original prize pool: $4M (Team Atlanta), $3M (Trail of Bits), $1.5M (Theori). Follow-on $1.4M for the 7 finalists to integrate into critical infrastructure. ARPA-H committed $20M for transition
- **What it funds:** Open-source cyber reasoning systems. All 7 finalist systems are OSI-licensed — fertile ground for kastil-systems to integrate / extend / co-author
- **Eligibility:** AIxCC competition closed. Follow-on track: open competitive solicitations expected in '26-'27 for adoption by critical infrastructure operators
- **Strategic value:** Even *adopting* the AIxCC open-source stack into kastil tooling generates publishable evidence + signals into DARPA for the next round
- **Source:** https://www.darpa.mil/news/2025/aixcc-results · https://aicyberchallenge.com/finals-winners-announcement/

### 1.4 DARPA I2O Office-Wide BAA (general)
- **Funder:** DARPA I2O
- **Award size:** $0.5M–$15M typical; unsolicited proposals welcomed
- **What it funds:** Anything aligned with I2O priorities (cyber, AI, formal methods, secure systems)
- **Eligibility:** US firms preferred; foreign nationals OK on certain efforts
- **Process:** Rolling. Short white paper first (5-15 pp), then invited full proposal
- **Source:** https://www.darpa.mil/research/opportunities/baa

### 1.5 AFRL Information Directorate (Rome, NY)

### 1.8 DHS S&T Silicon Valley Innovation Program (SVIP)
- **Funder:** DHS Science & Technology Directorate
- **Award size:** **Up to $2M non-dilutive over 24 months**, phased
- **What it funds:** Prototype projects from startups + small businesses for DHS components (CISA, Privacy Office, Cyber-Active-Defense, Synthetic Data); explicit cybersecurity emphasis
- **Eligibility:** **U.S. AND international startups eligible** (one of the rare federal openings to foreign firms)
- **Process:** Topic-call response model. Watch dhs-silicon-valley@hq.dhs.gov for calls
- **Strategic value:** Non-dilutive + operational-component customer in the same envelope. Very high

### 1.9 Defense Innovation Unit (DIU) — Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO)

- **Eligibility:** Requires academic affiliation. *Path for Joey:* partner with a CAE-R university (UMD, GMU, Purdue, RIT) as PI's contractor
- **Source:** https://www.nsa.gov/Academics/Centers-of-Academic-Excellence/

### 4.1 **Sovereign Tech Fund (STF) / Sovereign Tech Agency — VERY HIGH FIT**
- **Funder:** German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Climate Action (BMWK)
- **Award size:** Minimum cost **€50,000**; KDE recently received **€1M+**; no public cap
- **What it funds:** Open digital base technologies (libraries, foundational infrastructure). Security audits, conference attendance can be bundled. **Exactly the libsecurity.a pitch**
- **Eligibility:** **Any nationality; project just needs to be OSS with permissive/copyleft license**. Joey qualifies if kastil-systems publishes libsecurity.a as OSS

- **Strategic value:** **Best-in-world funder of foundational OSS infrastructure-security. Joey's "pure C99 / no deps / private PKI" pitch is the platonic STF target.** This is the single highest-fit funder in the entire scan
