§1 — Five concrete use cases

UC-1 — Forensic dispute resolution (medium probability)

Scenario. Singapore prime returns a fact contradicting field ground truth. Joey needs to prove whether Singapore's store was compromised, a relay allowed a rogue write, or transit was spoofed.

What audit catches. "Who wrote this fact, when, from where. Was it modified post-write." Without audit you can query the current store but cannot prove state at 21:45 Tuesday.

Operator action. ~wtf --retro --node singapore → audit_walk() returns 3 entries (WRITE Ashburn → SHARE relay-la → VERIFY Singapore). audit_verify() confirms chain unbroken. Dispute resolved forensically.

(action=WRITE,  obj_id=c7d42e9f..., actor=prime-ashburn,    ts=21:45:32, prev_hash=...)
(action=SHARE,  obj_id=c7d42e9f..., actor=relay-la,         ts=21:46:01, prev_hash=hash1)
(action=VERIFY, obj_id=c7d42e9f..., actor=prime-singapore,  ts=21:46:15, prev_hash=hash2)
(action=READ,   obj_id=c7d42e9f..., actor=nous-desktop,     ts=21:47:09, prev_hash=hash3)

Without audit. Disputes over data provenance become unresolvable. Hostile/confused operator can claim "the relay spoofed it" with no way to prove or disprove.

UC-2 — Federated sharing audit (HIGH probability)

Scenario. Joey shares intel dataset with a partner NOUS instance (law enforcement, humanitarian org). Partner demands cryptographic proof the dataset wasn't altered in transit or at our end.

What audit catches. Court-admissible chain: every SHARE event + exact timestamps + signature chain. Each entry cryptographically signed; append-only log = legally defensible.

Operator action. mesh_share("dataset-123", partner_id) calls audit_append(SHARE) per fact. Partner's local audit appends VERIFY. Joey + partner both run audit_verify() independently. PDF export with hashes embedded.

(action=WRITE,  obj_id=5a1c3e4..., actor=joey,           ts=09:00:00, prev_hash=...)
(action=SHARE,  obj_id=5a1c3e4..., actor=joey,           ts=09:00:15, prev_hash=hash1)
(action=RELAY,  obj_id=5a1c3e4..., actor=relay-la,       ts=09:00:45, prev_hash=hash2)
(action=VERIFY, obj_id=5a1c3e4..., actor=partner-nous,   ts=09:01:30, prev_hash=hash3)

Without audit. No cryptographic proof. If partner challenges the dataset in court, no immutable record of when facts were written, by whom, when they left the system. Legally indefensible.

UC-3 — Field intel chain of custody (HIGH probability)

Scenario. Field operator captures photo + audio + document photo via nous-desktop. Court or oversight board demands proof of provenance device → mesh → analyst, with no modification along the way.

What audit catches. Hash of media at capture + relay timestamps + prime VERIFY + every analyst READ with user_id. Full chain walkable + signed.

Operator action. Field op captures → Director calls audit_append(WRITE) immediately. Mesh syncs (SHARE → RELAY → VERIFY). Analyst runs ~retro --media-audit photo_hash → audit_walk() returns full chain. Exported as evidence file.

(action=WRITE,  obj_id=9b2f1a8..., actor=field-op-delta, ts=14:23:07, prev_hash=...)
(action=SHARE,  obj_id=9b2f1a8..., actor=nous-desktop,   ts=14:23:15, prev_hash=hash1)
(action=RELAY,  obj_id=9b2f1a8..., actor=relay-la,       ts=14:24:02, prev_hash=hash2)
(action=VERIFY, obj_id=9b2f1a8..., actor=prime-ashburn,  ts=14:24:30, prev_hash=hash3)
(action=READ,   obj_id=9b2f1a8..., actor=analyst-joey,   ts=15:10:00, prev_hash=hash4)

Without audit. Cannot prove capture time, no proof of non-modification, no chain of analyst access. Opposing counsel argues tainted evidence. May be inadmissible.

UC-4 — Compliance reporting (VERY HIGH probability)

Scenario. Regulator (GDPR, HIPAA, CFPB, SEC) demands "show every access to dataset Z in last 90 days." 90-day retention. Must be tamper-evident + auditor-verifiable.

What audit catches. Every READ event with actor_id + timestamp + prev_hash. Regulator can independently verify chain to confirm authenticity.

Operator action. ~compliance --dataset Z --days 90 → audit_walk(obj_id=Z, action=READ, since=-90d). Returns 47 entries. Generates signed PDF embedding hash chain. Regulator runs audit_verify() on the chain. Chain unbroken = report authentic = compliance closed.

(action=READ,   obj_id=dataset-z, actor=alice,           ts=2026-05-15T09:32, prev_hash=...)
(action=READ,   obj_id=dataset-z, actor=bob,             ts=2026-05-15T14:11, prev_hash=hash1)
(action=READ,   obj_id=dataset-z, actor=joey,            ts=2026-05-18T08:00, prev_hash=hash2)
(action=DELETE, obj_id=dataset-z, actor=system-retention,ts=2026-08-20T00:00, prev_hash=hash4)

Without audit. Regulators ask: "How do I know you didn't edit this log?" Without append-only + hash-chained = report rejected. Fines/enforcement.

UC-5 — Incident replay (medium probability)

Scenario. Tuesday 14:32 Singapore store starts returning wrong answers. Detected 16:00. Root cause at 22:00: corrupted index. Joey needs to know which facts were served corrupted during 14:32–16:00 window.

What audit catches. Forward walk from 14:32 → 16:00 shows which facts were READ + SHARED while corrupted. Surgical remediation possible.

Operator action. audit_walk(t_after=14:32, t_before=16:00, action=[READ,SHARE]) → "Facts X, Y, Z read 12 times each by alice/bob/carol; Z shared to Ashburn at 15:47." Alert team: "Discard decisions made on X/Y/Z post-14:32."

(action=WRITE,  obj_id=fact-x, actor=alice,           ts=2026-05-21T14:20, prev_hash=...)
(action=READ,   obj_id=fact-x, actor=alice,           ts=2026-05-21T14:32, prev_hash=hash1)  ← corruption window
(action=READ,   obj_id=fact-x, actor=bob,             ts=2026-05-21T14:45, prev_hash=hash2)
(action=SHARE,  obj_id=fact-x, actor=analyzer-ai,     ts=2026-05-21T15:47, prev_hash=hash3)
(action=DELETE, obj_id=fact-x, actor=system-restore,  ts=2026-05-21T22:15, prev_hash=hash4)  ← resolved

Without audit. Restore from backup, hope it's good. Don't know corruption window or downstream impact. Days lost figuring out scope.
