Digital Self

Story archive and identity page for joeycastillo.us -- the template for every user's FQDN

Intent

Your FQDN is your identity -- more authoritative than any government-issued document, email, or tag number. The page at joeycastillo.us should reflect the full depth of who you are: what you've built, what you've learned, what you're proud of, and the story of how you got here.

This isn't a page you sit down and author. It's a living accumulator -- you tell stories at any time, they get cataloged as triples, placed on a timeline, and the page reflects your evolving identity. The more stories, the richer the self.

Everything built here becomes the template for every user of the platform. Having a personal FQDN is a requirement to use the tools. This is the reference implementation.

Scope

In Scope

  • Story archive -- stories/ directory with JSON triple files, universal schema
  • Identity page rebuild -- curated front page showing featured triples, identity declaration, stats
  • Timeline page -- all stories rendered chronologically from the archive
  • Category lenses -- filter views: sport, creation, lesson, recognition, story, growth
  • Seed stories -- initial archive from known data (CLAUDE.md quotes, bio, existing profile)
  • Ingestion convention -- documented flow for adding stories via conversation

Out of Scope (future)

  • People graph (auto-render from people[] field) -- future board
  • Template extraction for other users -- future board after Joey's is solid
  • Machine-readable identity contract (platform reads FQDN) -- future
  • Era definition interview -- Joey defines his eras as stories accumulate

Approach

The Triple -- Universal Shape

{what, why I'm proud, era}

Every entry in the archive is a triple with metadata. Works for everything:

  • Sport: {hit .387 All-Conference, outworked everyone, high-school}
  • Creation: {billion-hour POS platform, outlived the company, enterprise}
  • Lesson: {can't argue with right, changed how I fight, javelin}

Story Schema

{
  "triple": {
    "what": "description of what happened or was achieved",
    "proud": "why this matters -- the significance",
    "era": "named era of life"
  },
  "category": "sport|creation|lesson|recognition|story|growth",
  "date": "YYYY or ~YYYY or YYYY-MM-DD or null",
  "people": ["Name One", "Name Two"],
  "domain": "code|leadership|life|business|hardware|athletics|people",
  "tags": ["tag1", "tag2"],
  "featured": true
}

Ingestion Flow

  1. Joey tells a story in any conversation
  2. AI extracts the triple + metadata
  3. AI reads back: "Got it -- {what, proud, era}. Featured?"
  4. Joey confirms
  5. File lands in stories/, named by date + slug
  6. Page rebuilds on next ship

Identity Page Structure

  1. Declaration -- "Build Your Digital Self" + name + one-line identity statement
  2. Stats bar -- 40+ years, 1B+ hours, N stories (live count from archive)
  3. Featured stories -- curated triples rendered as cards, grouped by category
  4. Platform links -- ai-pantheon.ai, /metal, metal-desktop, nous
  5. Philosophy -- "How I Think" section from lesson triples
  6. Timeline link -- "See the full story" link to timeline.html

Timeline Page Structure

  1. Reads all JSON files from stories/ at page load
  2. Sorts chronologically
  3. Renders as vertical timeline with era markers
  4. Category filter buttons at top (all, sport, creation, lesson, recognition, story, growth)
  5. Each card shows the triple, date, tags, people

Phases

Phase 1: Foundation

Story schema, seed the archive with known stories from CLAUDE.md and existing profile data. Create the stories/ directory with initial JSON files.

Phase 2: Identity Page

Rebuild index.html as the Digital Self page. Declaration, stats, featured story cards, platform links, philosophy section. Sidebar nav: H (Home/Identity), T (Timeline), reads from stories/.

Phase 3: Timeline

Build timeline.html -- reads all story JSONs, renders chronological vertical timeline with era markers and category filters. Client-side JS, no build tools.

Phase 4: Polish + Convention

Document the ingestion convention. Test the full flow: tell a story, catalog it, see it render. Responsive design pass. Verify Cloudflare Pages deployment.

Constraints

  • No frameworks. Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS only.
  • No build tools. Static site, served as-is from Cloudflare Pages.
  • All stories are JSON files in stories/ -- client-side JS reads them.
  • Dark theme: #0a0a0a background, #6FC3DF accent (existing design system).
  • Mobile-first responsive.
  • Template-ready: the structure must work for any user's FQDN, not just Joey's.
  • Existing pages (/metal, /dev, /meet, /retros) remain untouched.
