Why This Exists

Joey convinced Art Julian to own the code (1997/98) and then built the result: NorthStar, CBS's POS platform. This dossier maps what NorthStar is today — its products, its integration surface, and the customers running on it — from the public record on cbsnorthstar.com and third-party sources. It's the receipts behind "I built the entire restaurant stack." Joey annotates each engagement with his personal role.

The tell of a platform built right: Joey has been gone a while, and NorthStar is still shipping new versions, signing new chains, and — right now — rolling into the Cheesecake Factory and BJ's. Code outlives its author only when it was built to.

Attribution — read this first

CBS ran two distinct lines. Keep them separate.

CBS's business splits three ways — only two of them are Joey:

1. Legacy CBS reselling + hardware (NOT Joey). Reselling POSitouch (the core POS product, which is RDC's) plus restaurant hardware sales & installation. CBS's older dealer business.

2. The enterprise software layer on POSitouch (= Joey). POSitouch had no enterprise. Joey built the layer that made it sellable to big chains — reports, change management, integrations, large-data access, mining, data cubes, alerts. This is what closed CBS's major deals.

3. NorthStar, the owned POS (= Joey, lead architect). The full point-of-sale platform — funded by Stacked (CBS paid them back), built by Joey. Decision split: customer defines what/why, Art Julian owns POS-side business calls, Joey makes every technical decision.

Note: NorthStar integrates with POSitouch, MICROS, and Aloha (see Integration Surface) — interop, not authorship. Buckets 2 & 3 are Joey's software; bucket 1 is CBS's legacy reselling/hardware.

Headline — the platform is landing at the giants (now)

NorthStar POS has been signed and is rolling into The Cheesecake Factory and BJ's Restaurants. ● Joey, first-hand — even after leaving, the POS he architected is going into two of the largest casual-dining names in America. ● public corroboration — CBS's BJ's relationship is documented: CBS ran one of the industry's largest handheld deployments for BJ's (3,800 devices across 195 locations) and was named BJ's 2017 Supplier of the Year.
