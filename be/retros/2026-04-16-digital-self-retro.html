What Happened

Full ~GO chain executed on joeycastillo.us. Transformed a simple landing page with 3 links into a living digital identity backed by a story archive. The concept: your FQDN is your identity, and it accumulates as you tell stories over time.

Chain steps: ~p (plan) -- ~e (estimate) -- ~b (Board 1: 18 pts) -- ~t (18/18 pass) -- ~b (Board 2: 13 pts) -- ~s (ship) -- ~r (this retro)

What Went Well

  • Clean architecture. The triple format {what, proud, era} is simple, universal, and extensible. Works for sports, code, lessons, recognitions -- everything.
  • Single-fetch rendering. stories/index.json as a compiled manifest means both pages load with one fetch. No directory listing needed on a static host.
  • Discussion first. The concept discussion before ~GO shaped the entire design. The "proud of" filter, the featured/timeline split, the ingestion flow -- all emerged from conversation.
  • Template-ready from day one. Nothing in the code is Joey-specific except the content. Any user's FQDN can use this exact structure.
  • No frameworks, no build tools. Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS. Deploys as-is to Cloudflare Pages. Exactly how Joey builds.

What Could Be Better

  • Shell cwd resets. Every cd to joeycastillo.us got reset back to C:\metal. Worked around with full paths and cd-then-command chaining, but it slowed the chain.
  • Board 2 overlap. Items 1 (sidebar nav) and 4 (live stats) were already built as part of Board 1. Better estimation would have caught this -- those items should have been part of Board 1 or not sized separately.
  • No browser test. Validated structure and content programmatically but didn't launch a server for visual verification. The Cloudflare Pages deploy is the real test.
  • Eras not yet defined by Joey. Used placeholder eras (early, javelin, enterprise, metal) from CLAUDE.md. The real era names should come from Joey as stories accumulate.

Commitments

  • When Joey tells a story, use the ingestion convention: extract triple, confirm, file, index, ship.
  • Let eras emerge organically -- don't force them.
  • Sports stories are the next big category to populate -- Joey mentioned seasons, records, accomplishments.
  • People graph (auto-render from people[] field) is future scope -- will become valuable as the archive grows.
