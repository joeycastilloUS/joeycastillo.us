The Signature

~d · joeycastillo.us trust-first revamp · one name on everything he ships

1 · Subject + Intent

Revamp joeycastillo.us to sell the man, not the company. The name is the brand; the brand is the warranty. Make a stranger trust "Joey Castillo" enough to use — and eventually pay for — an app published under that name, before they've ever met him. The digital self is the first product, and it is the builder himself.

2 · Discovery

The site already has the right bones: kastil palette, thin type, a digital-self engine (identity + timeline). But the engine is empty (stories/index.json = [] → homepage shows zeros), the headline sells a product abstraction ("Build Your Digital Self"), the company platform list sits in prime real estate, and every piece of real delivery is password-gated. Meanwhile every trust primitive already exists in tree — transparency (public retros/issues), a named client (Mendocino Farms), zero-knowledge crypto (TLF), and a live /meet room. The problem is not missing assets. It is illegibility: none of it reaches a stranger in ten seconds, and the proof tank is empty.

4 · Shape

Minimal, trust-first home — three beats above the fold, proof on one scroll. Keep palette, thin type, mono accents, favicon.

Above the fold (3 beats)

  • The name — Joey Castillo, large, thin (unchanged).
  • The claim — replaces the product tagline: "40 years building the systems you never had to think about." Sub: "A billion hours of uptime. Land and sea. The name is finally on the door."
  • The signature line — "Everything I publish here ships under one signature."

One scroll of proof (in order)

  • The record — the populated timeline (eras + real systems + the names you'd know). The empty tank, filled.
  • What I publish — each app card stamped shipped by joeycastillo.us (the maker's mark).
  • The receipts — "I publish my retros, issues, and failures. Read them." Links into the transparency surfaces.
  • Why your data is safe — one line on zero-knowledge (TLF), for the payment fear.
  • Meet now — the live door.

Out of scope (this pass)

  • The company platform stack (kastil / nous / pantheon / metal desktop) — demoted to a single quiet "what I'm building now" line, far down. Not the headline.
  • Payment integration itself. This pass builds the trust that makes payment possible; checkout is a later board.
  • Ungating client work. Proof comes via public case-study faces, not by exposing gated deliverables.

5 · The Story (the artifact's core)

You've used my software. You just never knew my name.

For forty years I built the systems nobody thinks about — the ones that just have to work. I started writing code at seven. By the time the restaurant and hospitality world ran on point-of-sale and back-office reporting, my code was underneath it: taking the order on a Saturday-night rush, closing the books at 2am, running the same on a ship at sea as a counter on land. Over a billion hours of combined uptime. You've almost certainly stood in front of a screen running my work. You never saw my name. That was the job.

I did it for almost everyone — the names you'd know. Many languages, hardware and software, land and sea. Mostly restaurants and hospitality, because that's the hardest real-time, lowest-margin-for-error environment there is. Survive a Friday dinner rush and you can survive anything. The restaurant floor was just the proving ground; the discipline is the same everywhere — build the thing that doesn't break, ship it, let it run.

Now I'm doing something I never did before. I'm putting my name on the door.

joeycastillo.us isn't a company. It's me. After four decades of my code carrying other people's names, the name on the work is finally my own. Everything I publish here ships under one signature. If it says joeycastillo.us, it carries the same standard that ran a billion hours without you ever noticing. That's not a slogan. It's a warranty.

This is the first digital self for sale. Not the company. The builder.

⚠ Content dependency: the "names you'd know" track record is Joey's to supply — never fabricate employers or clients. The story above uses only categories Joey stated (restaurant/hospitality POS + reporting, land/sea/air, 40 years, billion hours). Named specifics land when Joey provides the list.

6 · The Trust Stack (how a stranger trusts before paying)

1
The name is the warranty. Every app carries a visible "shipped by joeycastillo.us" mark linking back to the man and the record. They trust who signed it, not the app.
2
Receipts, not promises. Public retros, issues, failures. Can't be faked. Lean all the way in.
3
The record, named. Fill the timeline with the real eras and systems. The proof that converts. #1 content job.
4
Zero-knowledge architecture. TLF: "you don't have to trust me with your data — I built it so I can't betray you." Math beats testimonials for the card-number fear.
5
The human. /meet — a live door. Talk to me now. Oldest trust signal there is.

8 · Escape Routes

  • Man-not-company — reversible: the company platform line still exists, just demoted. Re-promote anytime.
  • The Signature naming — cosmetic; rename without structural cost.
  • Maker's mark on apps — additive overlay; removable per-app.
  • Publishing failures — load-bearing & hard to reverse: once public, retros are seen. Mitigate by choosing which surfaces go public; the discipline stays, the exposure is scoped.

Pure Metal Test
