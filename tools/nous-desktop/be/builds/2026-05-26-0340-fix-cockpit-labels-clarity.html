Why two dropdowns?

Both default to claude-opus-4-7, so you see the same model name twice. They're two independent settings — change one without affecting the other.

  • MAIN AI — the headline. When you type "explain this code" or any normal question, this AI handles it. The full SENSE/SELECT/REQUEST trace fires in the THINKING pane.
  • SUMMARY AI — the sidekick. When you type /summarize (compare responses across all the fan-out tiles) or /advise (have one AI advise on another's response), this AI handles those specific commands. Doesn't fire on regular questions.

You can have MAIN AI = claude-opus-4-7 (smart, slow) and SUMMARY AI = gpt-mini (fast, cheap) — that's why the split exists. Or have them both be Claude if you don't care.

What "Let NOUS decide" means

Both dropdowns have a "Let NOUS decide" option. When picked: the NOUS mesh (your home-rack brain) decides which AI based on the query. But the mesh isn't live yet — so picking "Let NOUS decide" for MAIN AI today = THINKING pane fails silent with "NOUS unavailable (mesh down) — no driver". That's intentional honesty: when NOUS does nothing, the cockpit does nothing rather than secretly fall back to gemini.

Default of claude-opus-4-7 is the right pick until NOUS is real.
